First Light — Cosmic Microwave Background

The universe cooled enough for electrons to bind to nuclei, making it transparent for the first time. Photons streamed freely — the oldest light in existence, now detected as the Cosmic Microwave Background at 2.7 K.

The Cosmic Dark Ages

No stars existed yet. The universe was filled only with neutral hydrogen clouds, slowly clumping under gravity. Darkness reigned for hundreds of millions of years as the first seeds of structure were quietly sown.

First Stars — Cosmic Reionization

Population III stars — enormous, blazing hot, and short-lived — ignited for the first time. Their ultraviolet radiation reionized the surrounding hydrogen gas, ending the Dark Ages and lighting up the cosmos permanently.

First Starlight

T = 1 – 3 billion years

First Galaxies & Quasars

Gravity assembled gas and star clusters into the earliest galaxies. Supermassive black holes powered brilliant quasars. The cosmic web of filaments and voids began to take shape across the expanding universe.

Galaxy Formation

Our Solar System & Present Universe

From the ashes of earlier stellar generations, our Sun ignited 4.6 billion years ago. Earth formed shortly after. Today, an estimated 2 trillion galaxies populate the observable universe, which continues expanding — and accelerating — driven by dark energy.
