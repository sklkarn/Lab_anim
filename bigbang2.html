Map of the Universe
from singularity to now  ·  13.8 billion years
💥
Era I
The Big Bang
t = 0  ·  Temperature: 10³² K
The universe begins — not an explosion into space, but an explosion of space itself. All matter, energy, space, and time emerge from an infinitely dense singularity. The four fundamental forces are unified as one.
Singularity Planck Epoch 10⁻⁴³ s
🌀
Era II
Cosmic Inflation
10⁻³⁶ – 10⁻³² seconds  ·  Expansion: ×10⁵⁰
The universe undergoes exponential expansion — faster than light — driven by a quantum field called the inflaton. Tiny quantum fluctuations are stretched to cosmic scales, seeding the large-scale structure we observe today.
Inflaton Field GUT Symmetry Breaking
⚛️
Era III
Quark–Gluon Plasma
10⁻¹² – 10⁻⁶ seconds
A primordial soup of quarks, gluons, electrons, and their antimatter partners fills the universe. As it cools, quarks bind together to form protons and neutrons. For every billion antimatter particles, there are a billion and one matter particles — the slight asymmetry that saves existence.
Quarks Baryogenesis Matter Wins
🔵
Era IV
Big Bang Nucleosynthesis
1 second – 3 minutes  ·  T: 10⁹ K
Nuclear fusion ignites for the first time. Protons and neutrons fuse to forge the first atomic nuclei — 75% hydrogen, 25% helium, with trace amounts of lithium and deuterium. The elemental recipe of the cosmos is written.
Hydrogen 75% Helium 25% Nuclear Fusion
✨
Era V
Recombination & First Light
380,000 years  ·  T: 3,000 K
The universe cools enough for electrons to bind with nuclei, forming neutral atoms for the first time. The cosmic fog clears. Light travels freely — this ancient glow, stretched by expansion to microwave frequencies, reaches us today as the Cosmic Microwave Background.
CMB Universe Becomes Transparent
🌑
Era VI
The Cosmic Dark Ages
380,000 – 150 million years
The universe goes dark. There are no stars, no galaxies — only vast clouds of neutral hydrogen drifting through the void. Dark matter halos quietly accumulate under gravity, weaving the cosmic web that will become the scaffolding of all structure.
Dark Matter Cosmic Web Forms
⭐
Era VII
Cosmic Dawn — First Stars
150 – 800 million years
The first stars — Population III stars — ignite in the darkness. They are colossal, hundreds of times the mass of our Sun, blazing with blue-white fury. Their ultraviolet light tears apart the surrounding hydrogen, reionizing the universe. When they die as supernovae, they forge heavy elements for the first time.
Pop III Stars Reionization Heavy Elements Born
🌌
Era VIII
Galaxy Formation
800 million – 4 billion years
Galaxies coalesce along dark matter filaments, growing through collisions and mergers. Supermassive black holes ignite as blazing quasars at galactic cores. The Milky Way's ancestors begin to assemble. The universe blooms into its current large-scale structure — walls, filaments, voids — the cosmic web.
Milky Way Forms Quasars Dark Energy
🌍
Era IX
Our Solar System
9.2 billion years after Big Bang  ·  4.6 Gyr ago
From the remnants of ancient stellar explosions, a molecular cloud collapses. The Sun ignites. Dust and gas coalesce into planets. Earth forms, is struck by a Mars-sized body — the debris becomes our Moon. Oceans form, life emerges, evolves across billions of years toward complexity.
Sun Born Earth Forms Life Emerges
🔭
Era X  ·  Now
The Observable Universe — Today
13.8 billion years  ·  T: 2.725 K (CMB)
The universe spans 93 billion light-years in diameter, contains 2 trillion galaxies, and is expanding at an accelerating rate driven by dark energy — a mystery yet unsolved. You are made of stardust forged in stellar cores. You are the universe observing itself.
2 Trillion Galaxies 93 Billion LY Wide Accelerating Expansion You Are Here
