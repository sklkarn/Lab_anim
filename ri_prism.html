Experiment: Determination of Refractive Index of a Glass Prism

1. Aim

To determine the angle of minimum deviation for a given glass prism by plotting a graph between the angle of incidence and the angle of deviation, and to calculate the refractive index of the material of the prism.

2. Apparatus

  • Glass Prism
  • Drawing board, white paper, and drawing pins
  • Office pins (Alpins)
  • Protractor, ruler, and sharp pencil

4. Theory

The refractive index \(\mu\) of the material of the prism is calculated using the prism formula:

$$\mu = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{A + \delta_m}{2}\right)}{\sin\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}$$

Where A is the angle of the prism and \(\delta\)m is the angle of minimum deviation obtained from the graph.

5. Procedure

  1. Fix a sheet of white paper on the drawing board.
  2. Place the prism in the center and trace its boundary ABC.
  3. Draw a normal at a point on face AB and draw an incident ray at an angle i = 35°.
  4. Fix two pins P1 and P2 on the incident ray.
  5. Look for the images of these pins through face AC and fix pins P3 and P4 such that all four pins appear in a straight line.
  6. Remove the prism and join the pin marks to trace the emergent ray.
  7. Extend the incident and emergent rays to measure the angle of deviation δ.
  8. Repeat for angles 40°, 45°, 50°, 55°, and 60°.
