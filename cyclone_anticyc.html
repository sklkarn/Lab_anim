🌊 Ocean Origin

  • Forms over warm seas (≥ 26°C / 79°F)
  • Ocean evaporation powers latent heat release
  • Warm moist air rises rapidly near the surface
  • Strongest near coastlines — landfall weakens the storm

↑ Vertical Air Motion

  • Warm humid air rises in towering thunderstorm bands
  • Condensation releases latent heat — fuelling more ascent
  • Air diverges outward at high altitude (outflow)
  • Eye: cold air sinks, creating calm clear centre

🌀 Horizontal Circulation

  • Coriolis effect deflects inflow into a spin
  • Northern Hemisphere: counterclockwise rotation
  • Southern Hemisphere: clockwise rotation
  • Wind speeds 120–300 km/h in the eye wall

📊 Cyclone Anatomy

  • Eye — calm descending core
  • Eye Wall — peak intensity ring
  • Rain Bands — spiral convection
  • Ocean Surface — energy source

🌎 Land Origin

  • Originates over cold land masses, not over the ocean
  • Cold dense air sinks over land, building surface high pressure
  • Common over continental interiors and polar regions
  • Opposite of a cyclone — brings dry, calm, settled weather

↓ Vertical Air Motion

  • Air sinks (subsides) from high altitude downward
  • Descending air warms and dries — suppresses cloud formation
  • Results in clear skies, sunshine and low rainfall
  • Surface air diverges outward in all directions

↻ Horizontal Circulation

  • Coriolis effect produces clockwise spin (N. Hemisphere)
  • Southern Hemisphere: counterclockwise rotation
  • Gentle winds spiralling outward from the centre
  • Wind speeds much lower than cyclones (calm to moderate)

📊 Anticyclone Anatomy

  • Centre — warm sinking high-pressure core
  • Diverging Bands — outward air flow
  • Clear Sky — dry descending air
  • Sunshine — no storm clouds form
