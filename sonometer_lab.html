📖 THEORY & FORMULAE

A sonometer consists of a wire stretched over a wooden resonance board. When a tuning fork of known frequency is sounded and held over the wire, the wire can be made to resonate by adjusting the vibrating length L between the two movable knife edges.

At resonance, the frequency of the wire equals the frequency of the tuning fork. For the fundamental mode, a single antinode forms at the centre with nodes at both knife edges — the vibrating length L equals half the wavelength (λ/2).

T = MgTension from hanging mass
v = √(T/μ)Wave velocity in wire
λ = 2LWavelength (fundamental mode)
f = v/(2L)Fundamental frequency
f = (1/2L)√(T/μ)Mersenne's Law
f ∝ 1/L · √T · 1/√μProportionality relations
