Layer Key Facts
🔴 Thermosphere
Despite extreme temperatures, the gas is so thin that it feels cold. Home to the ISS (~400 km) and auroras. Absorbs X-rays and UV from the Sun.
🟣 Mesosphere
The coldest layer (−90°C at mesopause). Most meteors burn up here. Noctilucent clouds form at its top edge near 80 km altitude.
🔵 Stratosphere
Temperature increases with altitude due to UV absorption. Very stable — no weather here. Commercial aircraft cruise at its lower boundary (~10–12 km).
🟢 Ozone Layer
O₃ concentration peaks between 15–35 km. Absorbs 97–99% of harmful UV-B and UV-C radiation. CFCs caused a measurable hole over Antarctica.
🟩 Troposphere
Contains ~80% of atmospheric mass and nearly all water vapour. All weather occurs here. Temperature drops ~6.5°C per km of altitude gain (lapse rate).
