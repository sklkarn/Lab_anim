Lab Conditions

vtheoretical 343.4 m/s
formula 331.4 + 0.6T
Tube inner diameter 3.0 cm
end correction e = 0.3d 0.9 cm
corrected L₁′ — cm
corrected L₂′ — cm
End correction accounts for the antinode forming slightly above the open end of the tube. Corrected lengths: L₁′ = L₁ + e, L₂′ = L₂ + e. Since e cancels in L₂′ − L₁′, λ is unaffected — but individual corrected lengths are shown for accuracy.
Calculation
λ = 2(L₂ − L₁)
v = f × λ
vth = 331.4 + 0.6T
Procedure
1

Strike the tuning fork and hold it above the open end. It vibrates at a known frequency f.

2

Lower the water. At L₁, the air column = ¼λ — a standing wave forms. Sound is loudest. Note: the antinode forms slightly above the tube — add end correction e = 0.3d.

3

Lower further. At L₂, the column = ¾λ (2nd resonance). Difference L₂ − L₁ = ½λ. End correction cancels in this difference.

4

Compute λ = 2(L₂−L₁) then v = fλ. Compare to v = 331.4 + 0.6T at your lab temperature.
